# CARS Framework Reference
## Create a Research Space (Swales, 2004)

The CARS model describes three rhetorical moves that together establish the
space for a new research contribution. Each move builds on the last, creating
a funnel structure from broad field to specific contribution.

**IMPORTANT**: Apply this framework INVISIBLY. Never label paragraphs as
"Move 1", "Move 2", "Move 3". The output must read as natural academic prose.

---

## MOVE 1: Establish the Territory

**Purpose**: Demonstrate that the topic belongs to a recognised scholarly
conversation and that prior work exists worth building on.

**What to include**:
- Reference to seminal or recent work in the field
- Statement of the field's importance or relevance
- A narrative arc, not a list of facts

**Citation requirement**: Move 1 MUST contain at least 3-4 citations from the
reference table. These are the "territory" papers that establish what is known.

**Language signals** (Gray, 2018):
- Present perfect for general trends: "Studies have shown...", "Research has
  demonstrated...", "Scholars have argued..."
- Simple past for specific studies: "Smith (2019) found...", "Jones et al.
  (2021) showed..."
- Generic plural nouns: "studies", "researchers", "models" (not "a study")

**Failure mode** (Anderson, 2022):
If you can remove all sentences about prior studies and the text is still
readable, the introduction is a list of facts, not a logical argument.
Fix: add conjunctions that make the logic depend on the cited work.

**Linking conjunctions to use**:
- "However..." (signals a conflict or limitation)
- "Therefore...", "As such...", "Hence..." (signals a logical extension)
- "Furthermore...", "In addition..." (builds the case)
- "Nevertheless...", "Despite this..." (signals a pivot)

---

## MOVE 2: Establish the Niche

**Purpose**: Identify what is missing, conflicted, or unresolved in the
territory. This is the academic justification for your study.

**Citation requirement**: Move 2 MUST contain at least 2-3 citations that
reveal the gap. These papers show what has NOT been done or where conflict exists.

**Types of niche** (Anderson, 2022; UCLA Writing Center):
1. Knowledge gap: an important question that has not been answered
   The gap must be SIGNIFICANT. Novelty alone is not a justification.
   Provide an argument for why filling the gap matters.
2. Literature conflict: two bodies of work that reach opposing conclusions
   Do not merely document the conflict. Hypothesise a cause.
   The study must test whether that hypothesis resolves the conflict.
3. Methodological limitation: prior work has weaknesses your study addresses
   Explain why your approach is better, not just different.

**Language signals** (Gray, 2018):
- "However, [gap statement]..."
- "X has not yet been studied / has not been adequately investigated"
- "Evidence for X is scarce / conflicting / limited"
- "Despite X, little research has examined Y"
- "The relationship between X and Y remains poorly understood"

**The Magic Trick Test** (Anderson, 2022):
Give your Move 2 to a naive reader with Move 3 removed. Ask them:
"What experiment do you think this paper is about to do?"
If they can predict it accurately, your Move 2 is strong.
If not, the gap statement needs to be more specific and directed.

---

## MOVE 3: Occupy the Niche

**Purpose**: State what your paper does to address the gap identified in Move 2.
Move 3 must directly respond to Move 2. It must not introduce a new topic.

**Citation requirement**: Move 3 should cite 1-2 papers that support your
methodological approach or data source.

**What to include**:
- Research question or hypothesis (explicit, not implied)
- Method and/or data source
- Scope: time period, geography, context
- Key findings (post-study) OR placeholder (pre-study)
- Roadmap sentence: "The remainder of the paper is structured as follows..."

**PRE-STUDY placeholder format**:
[PLACEHOLDER: In this paper, we [METHOD] to test the hypothesis that
[HYPOTHESIS]. Data: [SOURCE/DATASET]. Period: [DATES].
Replace this placeholder with actual findings when results are available.]

**Language signals** (SJSU Writing Center, 2023):
- "The purpose of this study is to..."
- "This paper examines / investigates / analyses..."
- "We use [method] to test the hypothesis that..."
- "The study contributes to the literature by..."

**Roadmap example**:
"The remainder of the paper proceeds as follows: Section 2 reviews the
literature, Section 3 describes the data and methodology, Section 4
presents results, and Section 5 concludes."

## The Funnel Structure

```
  BROAD
    |   Move 1: Field and seminal work (3-4+ citations)
    |   Move 1: Recent developments and trends
    |   Move 2: However, gap or conflict identified (2-3+ citations)
    |   Move 2: Why the gap is significant
    |   Move 3: This paper does X to address that gap (1-2 citations)
  SPECIFIC
    |   Move 3: Roadmap
```

**Minimum citation count for a complete introduction: 6-9 papers**
